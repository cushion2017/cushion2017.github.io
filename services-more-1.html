Movement Therapy

Movement therapy encourages you to use the movement of your body to promote your physical, mental, emotional and spiritual well-being. This allow the student to picture their fiscal strength in their mind, body or heart. Benefiting students reading abilities, concentration and behaviour.


Open class exercise


Part 1 | Take a deep breath in and reach your hand up and breath out. Fold your forearm down with your forehead resting on the table. Take your hands to the chair or table and bring your right foot back into a line so your left foot has a bend in it. Allow the students to find their balance point and bring their hands over their head and then straighten their arms


Part 2 | Now repeat that process this time bringing your left foot back in a line with your right foot slightly bent


Part 3 | Find a kickstand and bring your hands to your heart. Once you have that balance finds something to focus your eyes on and without moving grow your braces by expanding your arms


Part 4 | Now put your heads and arms on the table and picture yourself feeling strong. Ask the students their feelings, what are they experiencing, what the strength looks like, what are they telling themselves in their mind.


Part 5 | Get the students to right down their experiences of their positive thoughts.
